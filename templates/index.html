Understanding Breast Cancer

Breast cancer develops when abnormal breast cells grow uncontrollably, forming tumors that can spread if not detected early.

🎓

Global Impact

Breast cancer is the most common cancer among women worldwide, with 2.3 million cases and approximately 670,000 deaths globally.

⚙

Who Is At Risk?

Occurs in women of all ages after puberty, with rates increasing in later life. 99% occur in women, but men can also develop breast cancer.

★

Early Detection

In countries with effective screening programs, breast cancer mortality has dropped by 40% since the 1980s. Early detection is critical.

⚠

Risk Factors

Common risk factors include age (over 40), family history, obesity, alcohol use, radiation exposure, and genetic mutations (BRCA1, BRCA2).

✓

Survival Rates

When detected early and treated properly, breast cancer has high survival rates. Regular screening dramatically improves outcomes.

♥

Take Action

Regular self-examination and awareness of breast changes are crucial. If you notice unusual symptoms, seek medical advice promptly.
